ACAM / Crossbar Noise — Hardware SNR Analysis

Advisor briefing · DSE R = 128 – 1024 · ACAM = 130 rows (8-bit)

1. The question

As we sweep crossbar rows R in the DSE, does the hardware still support the 8-bit output precision the NL-DPE paper relies on? If not, at what R does it break, and what's the dominant mechanism?

2. Setup — three ACAM modes, two need study

ModeWhere it's usedIn scope?
log ↔ exp (paired) Attention core (score, softmax, wsum) NO — paired inverses cancel errors; softmax scale-invariance
Activation (ReLU/GELU) When done inline with ACAM NO — function-specific noise-aware training
ADC (identity quantiser) FC, Q/K/V, FFN1/FFN2 — general-purpose YES — output feeds CLB reduction + activation

Only ADC mode is "general purpose" — its output is just a number that goes into CLB reduction and then an external activation. The other modes are function-specific and can be trained offline to tolerate noise.

3. The four noise terms and why they scale differently

TermScalingPhysical reason
σideal (per-cell programming) √R Independent errors — variances add, σ grows as √N (central limit theorem)
σsneak (OFF-state leakage) √R Same family as ideal — independent per cell
σcorrelated (driver / supply / thermal) R Errors move together — σ's add linearly instead of in quadrature
IR drop (systematic signal loss) R² Wire resistance × wire current — both ∝ R, their product is R²
Why R² for IR drop, but √R for noise?
Noise = sum of independent random errors → variances add → σ scales as √R.
IR drop = (wire resistance ∝ R) × (wire current ∝ R) → deterministic product → ΔV scales as R².

4. The counter-intuitive result

Under ideal physics, more rows = LESS relative noise.
Signal grows ∝ R; independent noise grows ∝ √R; ratio SNR ∝ √R. Same reason longer camera exposures give cleaner images.

The "big crossbar is noisy" intuition in the IMC literature traces back to IR drop specifically — the only effect that scales faster than the ideal √R SNR gain.

The transition window is only ~280 rows wide. Precision stays flat at the ACAM cap, then falls off suddenly. It's a cliff, not a slope.

8. Quantization precision after CLB reduction (separate from analog noise)

Even with perfectly clean analog (no noise), the ACAM is still a fixed 8-bit quantiser. When V partial sums are summed in CLB, each one contributes its own ±½-LSB quantisation error.

σquant(per pass) = LSB / √12   |   LSB ∝ R
Cumulative after V passes: σtotal = √V · LSB / √12  ∝  √R · √V
At fixed K (= V·R): σtotal ∝ √(R·K)  ⟹  scales as √R

For an FC layer with K = 1024 inputs:

RV (passes)Per-pass LSBCumulative σEff. bits at FC output
12880.500.4111.3
25641.000.5810.8
51222.000.8210.3
102414.001.159.8
Going R = 128 → 1024 costs ~1.5 bits of FC-output precision, even with zero analog noise. The reason: per-pass LSB grows linearly with R, but cumulative quantisation error only averages by √V, so the LSB-growth wins.
Both R extremes are still above 8 bits — adequate for 8-bit inference targets after the activation requantises. Quantization-aware fine-tuning easily handles the residual ~1.5-bit headroom loss at R=1024.

9. Key takeaways

1. Within the DSE range (R = 128–1024), hardware SNR supports 8-bit output. The 130-row ACAM quantizer, not analog noise, is the binding constraint.
2. Reduction doesn't amplify noise in relative terms. V·H partial sums average: signal grows linearly, noise as √(V·H) → relative SNR improves.
3. The only R-dependent degradation mechanism is IR drop. It's a cliff, not a slope. Default α puts it near R ≈ 1800; aggressive wires (α = 10-6) move it to R ≈ 1024.
4. Tool measures hardware SNR only; end-to-end accuracy depends on NAF. Noise-aware fine-tuning is orthogonal to this analysis and handles activation kink sensitivity, quantisation-after-reduction, and cross-layer drift.

11. What to cite in the paper

"Under typical process parameters, hardware SNR at the ACAM output supports 8-bit discrimination across R ∈ [128, 1024] — the 130-cell ACAM quantiser is the binding constraint, not the analog noise floor. Cumulative quantisation noise after CLB reduction scales as √R at fixed K, costing ~1.5 bits of FC-output precision going from R = 128 to R = 1024 but staying above the 8-bit inference target. Analog SNR at the ACAM is R-independent in relative terms; the only hard R-dependent degradation mechanism is IR drop, which exhibits cliff-like behaviour around an α-dependent critical R. Final task accuracy is compensated by noise-aware fine-tuning as demonstrated in the NL-DPE paper."
