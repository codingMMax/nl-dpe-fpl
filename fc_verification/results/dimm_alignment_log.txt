=== Latency Alignment (tb_dimm_pipeline_latency.v) ===

Comparison unit: per DPE pass (= 1 score pair for K_id=2).
NOT amortized per-score — the DPE pass is the actual observable unit.

IMC Simulator (analytical pipeline model, no FSM constants):
  feed     = read_cycles + sram_lat × K_id
           = ceil(128×8/40) + 2×2 = 26 + 4 = 30 cycles
  compute  = ceil((core_ns - output_cycles × t_clk) / t_clk) = 4 cycles
  output   = ceil(128×8/40) = 26 cycles
  reduce   = ceil(log2(64)) = 6 cycles
  effective_output = max(output, reduce) = max(26, 6) = 26 cycles
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  per_pass = feed + compute + effective_output
           = 30 + 4 + 26 = 60 cycles

RTL Measured (per DPE pass):
  Score pair [0,1]: completed at cycle 66-67 (64 cycles from start)
  Score pair [2,3]: completed at cycle 131-132 (65 cycles from pair 0)
  Per DPE pass: ~65 cycles

Delta: +5 cycles/pass (FSM overhead not modeled analytically)
  - 3 cycles: controller delay (reg_full → nl_dpe_control)
  - 2 cycles: drain + score write

Analytical model correctly captures structural pipeline:
  - Feed phase includes SRAM read latency (2 cyc/identity block)
  - Reduction is pipelined with output (streaming accumulate → FREE)
  - No hardcoded FSM constants in simulator

Stage-by-Stage:
  | Stage             | Simulator | RTL    | Match |
  |-------------------|-----------|--------|-------|
  | Feed (read+sram)  | 30 cyc    | 30 cyc | ✓     |
  | DPE compute       | 4 cyc     | 3 cyc  | ~     |
  | DPE output        | 26 cyc    | 26 cyc | ✓     |
  | Reduce (streamed) | hidden    | hidden | ✓     |
  | FSM overhead      | 0         | 6 cyc  | (unmodeled) |
  | Per pass          | 60 cyc    | 65 cyc | +5    |

Note: The +5 delta is entirely from FSM-level overhead
(controller handshake + drain wait + score write) that the
analytical simulator intentionally does not model. Structural
pipeline stages (feed, compute, output, streaming reduce) all
match the RTL.
