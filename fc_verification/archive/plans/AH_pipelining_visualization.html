1. Attention Head Workload

1.1 Math primer

Q = X · W_Q       # N × d_model · d_model × d_head → N × d_head
K = X · W_K
V = X · W_V
S = Q · Kᵀ         # N × N score matrix
W = softmax(S)     # row-wise: exp(S) / sum_row(exp(S))
O = W · V          # N × d_head attention output

Why NL needs 4 DPEs per lane while AL needs 3 primitives per lane

OperationNL approachAL approachHardware difference
QKT (mac_qk) 1 ACAM crossbar (dimm_exp): K-identity packed 1 dsp_mac (K=16): 4 products/cycle NL: parallel bit-line eval. AL: serial K-tile.
exp(score) 1 DPE (sm_exp) ACAM mode 1 CLB-based LUT inside clb_softmax NL: analog exp via ACAM. AL: digital LUT.
1/sum (normalize) SAME DPE (sm_exp) mode-switched to inverse CLB-based inversion inside clb_softmax NL: ACAM mode-switching saves a DPE. AL: same module.
log(attn) 1 DPE (ws_log) ACAM (not needed — AL stays in linear domain) NL pays an extra DPE here for log conversion.
attn·V (mac_sv core) CLB add + 1 DPE (ws_exp) ACAM exp 1 dsp_mac (K=32) parallel-output NL: log-domain add + exp pair. AL: direct multiply.
Architectural rationale. NL avoids analog multiplication (expensive in ReRAM) by working in the log domain: a · b = exp(log(a) + log(b)). The cost is two extra DPE passes (ws_log + ws_exp) per mac_sv. AL stays in linear domain because digital MACs are cheap. This is why NL has 32 mac_sv DPEs while AL has 16 mac_sv dsp_macs — NL trades 2× cell count for analog efficiency per cell.

Why NL mac_qk (230 cyc) is 10× faster than AL mac_qk (2,304 cyc): Architectural — not engineering.

  • Same total work (counter Δ=0), different schedule. Counters confirm both archs do the same N²·d MAC operations.
